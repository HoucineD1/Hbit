# Hbit — Project Brief
### BMAD Phase 1 · Analyst Role

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Hbit** is a web-first personal growth platform that unifies six life domains — Habits, Sleep, State of Mind, Budget, Focus, and Planning — into one cohesive dashboard. Instead of juggling four or five disconnected apps, a user gets a single, beautifully designed space where every dimension of their self-improvement journey lives together and reinforces the others.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

### The Core Problem
Personal growth is fragmented. A person serious about improving their life uses a habit-tracking app, a separate budgeting tool, a sleep tracker, a mindfulness journal, and a task planner — each with its own login, its own data silo, its own learning curve. The cognitive overhead of maintaining multiple tools kills consistency, and consistency is everything in self-improvement.

### Pain Points (User-Validated)
| Pain | Impact |
|---|---|
| Too many apps to manage | Users abandon tools within 2 weeks |
| No cross-domain visibility | Lack of sleep affects focus — but no app connects the two |
| Apps feel clinical / utilitarian | Low emotional motivation to open them daily |
| Most premium apps are English-only | Francophone users are underserved |
| Progress feels invisible | Users don't see compound improvement over time |

### Why Now
The "all-in-one" trend in productivity (Notion, Linear, Arc) has proven that users will consolidate tools if the unified experience is polished enough. The personal growth space has not had a credible all-in-one player yet. The window is open.

## 3. Vision

> **"One dashboard. Every dimension of growth. Nothing left behind."**

Hbit is the app that serious self-improvers open every morning as a ritual — not out of obligation, but because it makes them feel in control of who they're becoming.

## 4. Target Audience

### Primary Persona — "The Motivated Generalist"
- **Who**: Anyone 18–40 who actively wants to improve their life
- **Mindset**: Growth-oriented, reads self-help content, has tried other apps
- **Frustration**: Starts strong then loses momentum because the tools don't make it easy to stay consistent
- **Dream outcome**: Feel like they have their life together across all areas — health, money, mind, time

### Secondary Persona — "Le Francophone Ambitieux"
- **Who**: French-speaking users (Quebec, France, Africa, Belgium)
- **Specific need**: A polished, bilingual tool — most competitors are English-only and feel foreign
- **Opportunity**: Underserved market with high growth potential, especially in Quebec and West Africa

### Anti-Persona (who Hbit is NOT for)
- Enterprise / team use cases
- Users looking for deep single-domain tools (dedicated accountants don't need Hbit's budget tracker)
- Users who want a gamified / "RPG your life" experience (Habitica owns that)

## 5. Market Landscape

### Competitive Overview
| Product | Strength | Weakness vs Hbit |
|---|---|---|
| Habitica | Gamification, engagement | Single domain, gamey aesthetic |
| Notion | Flexibility, all-in-one feel | No structure — you build everything from scratch |
| Streaks (iOS) | Habit tracking UX | iOS only, no other domains |
| YNAB | Best-in-class budget tracking | Single domain, expensive, steep learning curve |
| Calm / Headspace | Mindfulness depth | Single domain, no tracking integration |
| Apple Fitness+ | Premium feel, motivation | Fitness only, Apple ecosystem lock-in |
| Quittr | Clean dark UX, motivation-driven copy | Single domain (addiction quitting) |

### Hbit's Differentiators
1. **True all-in-one** — 6 domains in one app, with more coming
2. **Premium dark-mode aesthetic** — feels aspirational, not clinical
3. **Bilingual from day one** — EN + FR natively
4. **Web-first, no install required** — lower barrier to try
5. **Freemium** — users can get real value before paying

## 7. Business Model

### Freemium Structure

**Free Tier (Forever Free)**
- Access to all 6 modules
- Limited history (last 30 days)
- Basic charts and stats
- Single device sync

**Premium Tier (Hbit+)**
- Unlimited history and data export
- Advanced analytics and cross-module insights (e.g. "How sleep affects focus score")
- AI-powered daily summary and recommendations
- Multi-device sync
- Custom themes / accent colors
- Priority support
- Early access to new modules

### Pricing Target
- ~$6.99–9.99 CAD/month or ~$59.99 CAD/year (positioned below Headspace at $12.99/mo)
- Lifetime deal at launch for early adopters (~$79–99 CAD one-time) to build initial revenue

## 8. Success Metrics

### Acquisition
- Monthly active users (MAU)
- Landing page → signup conversion rate (target: >8%)
- Organic traffic from SEO and word-of-mouth

### Engagement
- D1, D7, D30 retention rates (target: D30 > 25%)
- Daily active users / Monthly active users ratio (DAU/MAU target: >0.4)
- Avg. modules opened per session

### Revenue
- Free-to-paid conversion rate (target: >4%)
- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

### Product Health
- Time-in-app per session
- Module completion rate (did they log something?)
- Churn rate

---

## 9. Assumptions & Risks

| Assumption | Risk if Wrong |
|---|---|
| Users want an all-in-one over specialized tools | Lower than expected adoption; mitigate by making each module best-in-class on its own |
| Freemium converts at 4%+ | Revenue doesn't cover costs; mitigate with lifetime deal early on |
| Web-first is viable | Mobile app competitors have better retention; mitigate by making PWA installable |
| FR market is meaningful | Low FR user count; mitigate by not over-investing in FR content before validating |
